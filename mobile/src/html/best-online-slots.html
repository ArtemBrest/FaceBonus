Bets, Variance & Rtp

The bet per spin cost is something that needs to be considered before any of the reels start spinning as it greatly affects how long your gaming session will last. Many of the online slot games now offer a wide range of betting limits and this helps all types of stakers to enjoy the experience and keep within their playing budgets. These cater to casual players with micro limits up to the higher limits that only the bankrolls of highrollers can afford. Games can come with lower limits starting at €0.05 or €0.10 per spin and these are perfect for casual stakers. While most slots come with limits in the region of €0.20 to €100-€200 per spin, these ranges can vary though with some games giving stakers the chance to play at €500 per spin and sometimes even more.

Stakers need to be mindful of bet limits because you can quickly spin through your bankroll if you don’t give any thought to how much you are playing each time. Payouts do grow considerably with the bigger spin amounts, but your gaming session will end a lot quicker if you do not correctly calculate your gameplay against your budget.

Variance is used to predict the level of risk when playing each slot game, and this is also commonly known as volatility in some parts of Stakersland. It is another important factor for you to remember to get the best possible slots experience from your available budget. Here is how it works. The low variance slot games are useful to casual players as they generally offer frequent but small, modest wins, or in other words low risk and low reward for relaxed casino fun. On the other end of the scale is the high variance slot games with their huge payouts that only occasionally get triggered, these offer high risk and high reward. Lastly, the medium variance slot game is often considered the safest bet for the typical staker and this is what we recommend overall. It is known amongst staker circles as the ‘golden zone’ with its ideal balance of risk and reward.

Lastly, the RTP, in simple terms, stands for the overall percentage that the game will pay back to all its players over its lifetime. In other words, it means over millions of spins and so this is not something that directly affects you individually, but it is worth bearing in mind along with the other factors mentioned here. For reference, the average RTP is set between 94-98% but jackpot slots will come with much lower RTPs due to the size of the prizes available to win. Lower RTPs mean more risk for bigger rewards which is exactly what you get with the best online jackpot slots that we listed above.

Bonus Rounds / Scatter Rounds

Many video slot games offer what are known as either bonus symbols or scatter symbols, but both of these terms represent one thing and that is access to a bonus round. Some games even come with both symbols and a quick read of the pay table will outline what each symbol is capable of. The main idea with bonus/scatter symbols is that you need to land three of them on the reels in order to activate the bonus round. By landing three of the symbols in one spin on any position across the reels, you will receive free spins or access to a unique bonus round, as well as bet multipliers. Landing four or five of the symbols will usually bring you even more free spins and bigger multipliers, too.

What Is An iGaming Provider?

In simple terms, an iGaming provider is a company that designs, develops, and supplies online games, slot games, and other gaming software products to the online casinos in operation across Stakersland. Some of these companies are standalone providers who solely create and rent their games out to casinos, while some of them have also stepped into the casino operating side of things as well. By creating the best online slot games and by introducing new and innovative slot features, gaming providers then create more demand from the staker communities which, in turn, helps them to gain more control over the priority positions in the casino lobbies.

How do the casinos and providers work together? The common setup between provider and a single casino is that the casino pays out an upfront fee to rent the games from the provider. An ongoing monthly fee is then negotiated, and this is based on the percentage of the revenue earned on those rented games. The monthly fee usually falls into around the 10-16% zone, but the bigger casinos are able to negotiate slightly better deals than those casinos who have only just opened their virtual doors. Game placement is also something that comes into play in these deals and a casino being known as a “NetEnt Casino” or “Playtech Casino” is going to prove worthwhile for both the provider and casino, which will no doubt help in any future negotiations on the fees being paid.
