Licensed RTP
The Licensed RTP is what would happen if you randomly make millions of spins on the game. This is the main RTP that casinos use to show the overall payout over the course of all bets on the game. Take note that it is not a guarantee for what you can win individually each time you play.
Actual RTP
The Actual RTP is what the game is currently paying out over a specific period of time. Technically, this RTP could either be above or below the Licensed RTP and it all depends on the amount stakers have wagered and won on the game in a recent time period, which is usually a month. This gives you a true representation of how the game is paying out right now and not the expected rate over the lifetime of the game, as per the Licensed RTP value.

How To Calculate Return To Player (RTP)

The RTP value may sound complicated but with a little explanation you will soon understand the concept behind it and, more importantly, how it can help you to find better slot games to play. So, let’s roll up our sleeves and get stuck into a few calculations to make some sense of this all-important RTP value.

To determine the Actual RTP of any game you need to divide the winnings and wagering figures. So, for example, take a game that has been designed with an RTP of 91.86% which then accumulates €1,200,000 in wagering and pays out a total of €1,085,000 in wins. The game RTP can be determined as such:

Winnings
1,085,000
/
Wagering figures
1,200,000
=
0.9042

This shows that the game has reached an Actual RTP rate of 90.42% which is actually under the Licensed RTP of 91.86%. This is not the end of the discussion though because volatility is also something that needs to be considered as it indicates the acceptable tolerance below or above the Licensed RTP. It should be understood right from the start that the overall tolerance will decrease the more the game is played.

So, you can expect to see the Licensed RTP and the Actual RTP to get equal or at least very close to each other over a substantial amount of time. This increase of RTP accuracy over time can be best explained with a table. We will assume that the game’s volatility is 5.6 which means that you can expect to the find the following lower and upper tolerance levels:

What this means is that even a non-faulty game will still have the chance to fall outside the +/- range once in every 20 tests. We could have opted to increase the confidence interval which would essentially minimise the risk of false alarms, but we do not want to make these tolerance levels too wide. Any confidence level over the 99% mark would mean that a non-faulty game may only fall outside the +/- range once in every 100 tests. The problem here is that one single measurement error does not necessarily mean that the game or the RNG mechanism is faulty. Only when you have consecutive or multiple errors over a given frequency can you then suggest a malfunction is evident.

Theoretical Win

Now for some interesting facts about how casinos use customer data…

The Theoretical Win (or Theo, for short) is the one value that helps casino marketing teams best describe and categorise a casino player. In short, Theo can be explained as the number that the casino expects you to win from the house and is calculated by taking the total wagering amount and the game that you played on.
