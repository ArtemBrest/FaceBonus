Wagering Requirements

Understanding the wagering requirements fully helps players accurately judge how much work and wagering is required to withdraw any winnings made from a casino bonus. Knowing that all your winnings will be locked until you hit that turnover amount starts to make things a lot more serious when claiming bonuses. As we mentioned earlier, the 35x amount is considered the industry standard. Anything below that amount is a high-value deal, and the deal provides less value as you go over and beyond that 35x marker.

Wagering requirements can often get confusing in the terms and conditions, and we often see bonus rules getting mixed up. This ultimately means that various bonus offers can end up being connected to the terms and conditions for a welcome bonus, meaning you end up getting unfair wagering requirements on free spins or other bonuses. As this happens a lot, we strive to only accept tested bonuses and casinos that make their terms and conditions fair and easy to follow. So, let’s highlight the next two points that help explain how a €2,000 wagering requirement total doesn’t have to sound impossible after all. Bonus Expiry

The bonus expiry window is directly linked to the wagering requirements rule. It shows you the length of time the casino is giving you to try and satisfy the wagering requirements. We see a lot of expiry periods being offered, from 24-48 hours to one week and up to one month. Let’s just say that the longer the casino gives you, the better it is for you and your chance of withdrawing your winnings. Try and stay clear of short 24-48 bonus expiry dates, with anything from two weeks or more as standard.

Eligible Games

The rules concerning eligible games come down to “weighting” and being able to play certain games that allow you to clear your wagering requirements faster. Casinos have their own rules for this, and so you will come across all types of weighting percentages. So, let’s drill down to the standard details we see the most. Slot games are often granted 100% weighting, meaning all your wagers on slot games will count towards clearing your wagering requirements. Table games and other games like jackpots often come with much lower weighting percentages, such as 5%-20%. It means only that percentage of every bet you make on those games will count towards clearing the wagering requirements. Ultimately, this means it takes longer and much more wagering to clear your bonus on table games than when wagering on slots.
